# Product

## Register

product

## Users

EcoPest serves pest-control field teams operating in Arabic-first, RTL workflows.

- Technicians work from phones in the field. They scan station QR codes, inspect bait stations, submit reports, save local drafts in the mobile app, and need fast recovery when camera, GPS, or network conditions are imperfect.
- Supervisors monitor daily operations. They review reports, follow shifts, attendance, tasks, payroll review, station activity, client orders, and exceptions without owning core setup.
- Managers own the operating system. They manage users, stations, reports, reviews, analytics, exports, audit logs, settings, and operational decisions that need traceability.
- Clients are a secondary audience through the client portal. They need clear account access, order visibility, and station/report context without seeing privileged internal controls.

## Product Purpose

EcoPest is an Arabic-first pest-control field operations suite anchored by bait station management. Its core workflow turns QR station visits, technician inspections, report review, attendance, tasks, client orders, analytics, exports, and audit history into one role-protected operational system.

Success means the team can see what happened in the field, what needs review, what needs follow-up, and who changed what without relying on informal messages or unverifiable spreadsheets.

## Brand Personality

Arabic-first, calm, precise, trustworthy, and operational. The product should feel like a serious field command tool: readable under pressure, restrained in presentation, and direct about status, errors, permissions, and next actions.

The voice is concise and practical. It should never sound like a marketing site, generic SaaS copy, or decorative AI-generated filler.

## Anti-references

- No decorative card farms.
- No empty marketing hero sections.
- No vague AI outputs without actions.
- No client-side privileged writes.
- No generic SaaS gradients, gradient text, glass defaults, or purple-blue launch-page styling.
- No nested cards, side-stripe status accents, or repeated icon-heading-text grids.
- No playful motion, animated spectacle, or custom affordances that slow standard admin work.

## Design Principles

- Put field speed first. Technician flows must minimize reading, typing, waiting, and recovery cost on mobile.
- Surface exceptions before raw lists. Dashboards should show pending reviews, stale stations, active shifts, failed sync, and follow-up work before general totals.
- Make status unmistakable. Station state, review state, sync state, attendance state, and permission state need consistent labels, colors, and placement.
- Preserve trust through server authority. Protected mutations stay role-gated, audited, and sourced from the session rather than client input.
- Keep density usable. Manager and supervisor screens can be data-rich, but hierarchy, filters, tables, and empty states must remain scannable.
- Treat dark mode as operational. It must be complete, readable, and useful for low-light field or dispatch work, not decorative.
- Keep mobile as the fast field companion. Secrets and privileged administration stay server-side while mobile focuses on scan, report, sync, and handoff.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA with field-first constraints. Preserve RTL throughout, keep Arabic as the default language, and handle LTR content explicitly for station IDs, emails, URLs, and technical identifiers.

Mobile field screens need at least 44px touch targets on web and 52px targets in the Expo app, readable 16px+ form text, visible focus/error states, high contrast status colors, and reduced-motion-safe interactions. Do not rely on color alone for operational status.
